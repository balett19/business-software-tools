The Business Software Life Cycle (BSLC) involves the creation, development, testing, and deployment of a software product or system. It begins with the requirements phase where lead developers or technical architects gather the required details for the proposed system and create a high-level design plan. This stage also helps define the objectives and scope of the product. Outputs of this phase include a project plan, cost estimates, and procurement requirements. The end result of the process is a functional and testable software product that is ready for deployment on the client's premises. The Business Software Life Cycle follows a structured methodology, which is typically divided into phases. Each phase has a specific purpose, so it is important to make sure the process is structured and documented properly. While SDLCs are not a one-size-fits-all process, they can help organizations develop and maintain software applications more efficiently. Using a Business Software Life Cycle can help you avoid wasting time and money on unnecessary steps in the process. By removing unnecessary steps, you can focus on delivering stable systems early, while still being flexible enough to quickly solve problems as they arise. Software development life cycles also allow teams to identify potential problems before they arise and eliminate them before they become a problem.

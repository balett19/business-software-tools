The Business Software Life Cycle (BSLC) involves the creation, development, testing, and deployment of a software product or system. It begins with the requirements phase where lead developers or technical architects gather the required details for the proposed system and create a high-level design plan. This stage also helps define the objectives and scope of the product. Outputs of this phase include a project plan, cost estimates, and procurement requirements. The end result of the process is a functional and testable software product that is ready for deployment on the client's premises. The Business Software Life Cycle follows a structured methodology, which is typically divided into phases. Each phase has a specific purpose, so it is important to make sure the process is structured and documented properly. While SDLCs are not a one-size-fits-all process, they can help organizations develop and maintain software applications more efficiently.
